## Why Naples as the closing base

- **Real Neapolitan pizza** — your boys love it, and the city is *literally* where it was invented (1889, Pizzeria Brandi). They get to live in pizza heaven for 14 days, eating at different historic pizzerias.
- **Cheaper than any other Italian city** — 30–40% below Rome
- **Centro Storico is gritty, authentic, working-class** — fits Dan's neighborhood brief perfectly
- **Greco-Roman archaeology** — Pompeii, Herculaneum, MANN museum (the world's best Greco-Roman artifacts)
- **Day trips to Capri, Procida, Amalfi, Atrani (Escher's village)**
- **November weather** is mild (mid-50s to mid-60s F), some rain, fine for everything
- **Less touristy** than Rome/Venice in November

## What's in Naples itself

### Naples sights
- **Cappella Sansevero** — Veiled Christ (marble veil over a body, looks impossibly soft) + crypt with the **Anatomical Machines** (real preserved bodies with full vascular system visible). Boys will lose their minds. Book ahead.
- **Napoli Sotterranea** — walk through Roman-Greek aqueducts + WWII bunkers with candles
- **MANN (Museo Archeologico Nazionale)** — world's best Greco-Roman collection, the stuff Pompeii preserved
- **Catacombs of San Gennaro** — early Christian underground complex
- **Castel dell'Ovo, Castel Nuovo, Castel Sant'Elmo** — three castles for the boys
- **San Gregorio Armeno** — the street of nativity figurines, year-round

### Weird-medieval Naples
- **Fontanelle Cemetery** — caves of skulls; Neapolitans "adopt" a skull and pray to it. Still active folk devotion.
- **Cappella Sansevero** (above) — the death cult masterpiece
- **Crypts of Santa Maria della Sanità** — underground baroque
- **Catacombs of San Gaudioso** — frescoed skull niches

### For the boys
- All three castles
- Sansevero anatomical machines
- Fontanelle skulls
- Pizza pilgrimage every night
- Pompeii day trip with a guide
- Ferry to Capri (Blue Grotto if weather allows)

## Amalfi + Atrani — Escher's village

Day trip or overnight. Atrani is a tiny village wedged between Amalfi and the next cliff — it's where M.C. Escher actually lived and drew. The maze of staircases that inspired his "Relativity" came from here.

- Train Naples → Salerno (40m)
- Bus or ferry Salerno → Amalfi
- Walk from Amalfi to Atrani (10 min along the coast)
- Have lunch in Atrani's tiny piazza
- Return via reverse

**Consider an overnight in Amalfi** to break up the round-trip travel (~$300 hotel).

## What's hard about Naples

- **Intense first impression** — scooters, noise, chaos, garbage piles. Takes 2–3 days to settle into.
- **Pickpockets** are real in tourist zones. Cross-body bags, no back-pocket wallets.
- **Driving is suicidal** — never rent a car here
- **Late-night noise in Centro Storico** — high floor, good windows matter
- **Some streets just feel sketchy** — trust your gut, especially at night
- **Naples is poorer than the rest of the route** — be aware of the contrast and be respectful
- **November weather** is variable — bring an umbrella and a real jacket

## What's amazing about Naples (the answer to all of the above)

It's the most alive city in Italy. The food is the best in Italy. The history is the deepest in Italy. The people are the warmest in Italy. Ending the trip here means leaving on a high note — every meal a memory.
