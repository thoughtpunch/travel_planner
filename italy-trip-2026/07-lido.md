## Why Lido, not Venice proper

- **Real neighborhood** — Lido di Venezia is a residential island with year-round locals, schools, supermarkets, a tobacconist, an actual barbershop
- **Venice is 12 minutes by vaporetto** — go when you want, leave when you're done
- **40–50% cheaper** apartments than Venice proper
- **Bikes are the main transport** — Lido is flat, ~11km long. Family bike rental for 3 weeks is gold.
- **No suitcase-dragging-over-bridges** like Venice
- **Quieter, easier sleep** — important for Dan's overnight work shift
- **Family roots angle** — this turns it into a "live here" experience, not a tourist stop

## What's in Venice (from Lido as base)

### Core sights
- **St Mark's Basilica + Square** — go at 8am or after 5pm to avoid crush
- **Doge's Palace + Bridge of Sighs** — pre-book, includes the **armory** (boys' favorite)
- **Rialto Market** — go at 8am when fish vendors are still working
- **Murano** — glassblowing demos, walk-up easy
- **Burano** — colorful fishing-island day trip (45m further vaporetto)
- **Libreria Acqua Alta** — surreal bookstore with bathtubs full of books

### Voynich / Escher / weird-medieval for Kei
- **Padua's Orto Botanico** (45m train) — **world's oldest botanical garden (1545)**. Plants from the actual Voynich Manuscript era. Don't miss.
- **Palazzo Fortuny** (Venice) — symbolist designer's house with weird textile and lamp installations
- **Sala dei Giganti at Palazzo del Te / Mantua** (1h 30m) — Giulio Romano's painted collapsing-room
- **Villa Pisani hedge maze** (Stra, near Padua, 1h) — one of Europe's most famous historic labyrinths
- **Sacro Monte di Varallo or Orta** (combined with Damanhur, longer trip) — bizarre 16th-c devotional sites with life-size painted plaster figures

### For the boys
- **Doge's Palace armory** — best armor collection in northeast Italy
- **Naval History Museum** (Venice) — boats, ships, naval cannons, models
- **Murano glassblowing demo** — hot glass + flame + magic
- **Bike around Lido** — endless freedom for older boys
- **Halloween in Italy** — now a thing, expect costumes + parties

## Dolomites weekend (recommended)

Rent a car from Venice Mestre (no cars in Venice). Drive up to **Val Gardena** or **Cortina d'Ampezzo**.

| Day | Plan |
|---|---|
| Fri | Drive from Mestre → base village (2.5h). Settle, walk, dinner. |
| Sat | **Tre Cime di Lavaredo** loop hike (10km, manageable for all ages) OR cable car summit |
| Sun | **Lake Braies** (Pragser Wildsee) row boats, turquoise water. **Earth Pyramids of Ritten** on the way back |
| Mon | Drive back to Mestre → Lido |

Costs: $200 rental + $80 fuel + $400–500 for 2–3 nights in a mountain lodge + meals.

**Use Chase Sapphire Preferred for the rental** — primary CDW coverage, decline the rental agency's insurance.

**Weather risk in late October:** check forecast 5 days before. If it's a wet weekend, replan as a Verona overnight instead.

## Halloween in Italy — Oct 31

Halloween has become a real thing in Italian cities, especially with American/international families. Venice runs costume events, kids trick-or-treat in restaurants, bars throw parties. Plan a costume day in Venice — book a fun late dinner (Estro, Antiche Carampane).

For something specifically weird: **the Venetian mask tradition** is older than Halloween. Visit a real mask-maker (Ca' Macana, Mondonovo) — boys can buy or make their own.

## What's hard about Lido / Venice

- **Late October gets cold and damp** — bring real coats. Acqua alta possible — apartment with floor 1+ matters.
- **Acqua alta** (high water flooding) — November is peak season. Manageable, even fun, but check forecast before St Mark's days.
- **Venice tourism crush** — much less in Oct/Nov than peak summer, but still real on weekends. Mornings beat afternoons.
- **Vaporetto with luggage** on arrival day — pre-arrange water taxi or porter on Lido side
- **Bringing groceries home from Venice** to Lido — better to grocery-shop on Lido itself (Coop, Conad)
- **Limited true sit-down dining on Lido** in October — many places close for the season after the Film Festival in early Sept
