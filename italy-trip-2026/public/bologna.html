— The shortlist

Bologna + Florence Top 10

Three weeks' worth, balancing the boys, the food, the art, and Grandma's mobility (porticoes are flat and covered with cafés every 30 feet).

  1. 1
    Motor Valley — Ferrari, Lamborghini, Ducati
    Maranello · Sant'Agata · Bologna

    The supercar pilgrimage: the Ferrari Museums pass, Lamborghini's live factory line (age 6+), Ducati in-city, plus Maserati and Pagani via Modenatur. Jude's heaven — full set on his page.

    All ages (factory tours 6–7+) · English · Jude's page
  2. 2
    Florence — Duomo, David, the Renaissance
    2–3 nights from Bologna

    Climb Brunelleschi's dome, see Michelangelo's David at the Accademia, wander the Oltrarno. The cultural heart Kei wants — sleep a couple of nights to do it justice.

    Adults love · all ages · English · duomo.firenze.it
  3. 3
    Grand Tour Italia (the old FICO food park)
    just outside Bologna

    The giant food park: pizza and gelato made in front of you, farm animals, rides, an indoor adventure park. A perfect Grey birthday day and an easy 9-year-old day.

    All ages · entry free · grandtouritalia.it
  4. 4
    Make fresh pasta — Taste Bologna
    central Bologna

    Hand-roll tagliatelle and tortellini, the region's signature skill (kids 4–10 get their own station). Sells out — book ahead.

    All ages · English · tastebologna.net
  5. 5
    Parmigiano dawn dairy + balsamic acetaia
    near Modena

    Watch the king of cheeses made at dawn, then taste barrel-aged balsamic (nothing like the supermarket stuff). The grown-up food morning; dairy is kid-friendly.

    Family · English · parmigianoreggiano.com
  6. 6
    Bologna itself — Piazza Maggiore + Asinelli Tower
    the porticoed old town

    Climb the leaning Asinelli tower, wander 40 km of UNESCO porticoes, eat at the Quadrilatero market. Grandma-friendly and free.

    All ages · tower book online · bolognawelcome.com
  7. 7
    Stibbert Museum — armor & armies
    Florence

    A hall of life-size mounted armored knights and a superb samurai collection — the boys' swords-and-armor jackpot. Pair with a Florence day.

    All ages · English · museostibbert.it
  8. 8
    Ravenna's Byzantine mosaics
    ~1 hr east · day trip

    Glittering 5th–6th-century gold mosaics in quiet little chapels — sacred geometry and otherworldly color, the parents' Escher/Voynich sweet spot. Dante's tomb too.

    Adults love it · English · ravennamosaici.it
  9. 9
    Cremona — the violin-making capital
    ~1.5 hr · day trip

    The Museo del Violino and lutherie workshops where Stradivari's craft lives on. The music thread for Rhys; you can hear a 1700s violin played.

    Teens + adults · English · museodelviolino.org
  10. 10
    Gelato Museum + Emilia castles
    Anzola · Torrechiara

    A hands-on family gelato masterclass at Carpigiani (English slots limited — book), plus the region's medieval castle circuit (armories, ramparts) for a weekend.

    All ages · English · gelatomuseum.com · castelliemiliaromagna.it
— More to do

Deeper cuts

Grown-ups love (Florence + Emilia)

Uffizi Gallery, Florence

Botticelli's Venus, Leonardo, the Renaissance canon. Book a timed slot; do a focused 2-hour highlights pass with the teens.

uffizi.it

Leather & mosaic workshops, Florence

Make a wallet at the Scuola del Cuoio inside Santa Croce, or set Florentine stone mosaic by hand. Real craft for older kids + adults.

scuoladelcuoio.it

La Specola mineral hall, Florence

Medici hardstones and the "Fiori della Terra" mineral show — great for the rock-loving teen and the design-minded parents.

sma.unifi.it

Family wins

Frasassi Caves day

~2.5 hr away: a huge lit cave hall for everyone, with an off-path speleo route (12+) for the older boys from the same site. See Adventures.

adventures.html

Scarperia knife town

A 700-year knife-making town in the Mugello: an interactive knife museum (all ages) and a forge demo for the teens. On the way to Florence.

adventures.html

Mineral museums, Bologna

Free Museo Bombicci (UV-fluorescent minerals, meteorites) beside a 26-m Diplodocus at Museo Capellini — a weekday-morning rock fix.

sma.unibo.it

Plan it

Book Maserati's factory tour first (it sets the Motor Valley calendar), plus Lamborghini, the Uffizi/Accademia slots, Asinelli Tower, and Carpigiani's English gelato class. Keep Grandma's window (Oct 1–14) to flat, central, café-rich days.
