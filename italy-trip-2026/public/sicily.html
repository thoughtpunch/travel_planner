Stop 3 · Sicily

Sicily — island of fire

Sep 21–28 · 7 nightsArrive Palermo by overnight ferry from NaplesLate Sept = best swimming

Greek temples better than Greece, an active volcano, golden Arab-Norman mosaics, street food, and warm September seas. EU under-18s enter every state site free, so the boys are free almost everywhere — you mainly pay for Etna, food tours and boats. Rent a 7–9 seat van and drive.

— The shortlist

Sicily Top 10

A counter-clockwise week from a Palermo arrival, ending near Taormina ready to head north. All verified June 2026.

  1. 1
    Mt Etna — cable car + Silvestri craters
    above Catania

    Europe's biggest active volcano. The cable car + Silvestri crater walk is all-ages; summit-crater hikes (~3,000m) are a 12+ add-on for the older boys + a parent. Buy legal lava/minerals in the shops — collecting is banned.

    Cable car all ages · English · funiviaetna.com
  2. 2
    Valley of the Temples
    Agrigento

    The best-preserved Greek temples on Earth, in an open archaeological park. Go early or at golden hour; big sunny walk.

    Under-18 free · English · coopculture.it
  3. 3
    Ear of Dionysius + Neapolis Park
    Syracuse

    A vast cave that booms when you clap (guaranteed 9-year-old win) beside a huge Greek theatre. Pair with Ortigia.

    Under-18 free · English · coopculture.it
  4. 4
    Teatro Antico, Taormina
    clifftop, framing Etna + sea

    A Greek theatre with arguably the best backdrop in the world. Short, high-reward; the town is a lovely evening stroll.

    Under-18 free · English · coopculture.it
  5. 5
    Palermo street-food tour
    Streaty · Day 1 off the ferry

    Arancine, panelle, sfincione through the Ballarò/Capo markets — the tastiest intro to the city. Adventurous eaters (no kids' menu).

    Family · English · streaty.com
  6. 6
    Arab-Norman mosaics — Monreale + Palatine Chapel
    Palermo

    Acres of gold Byzantine mosaic under Arab honeycomb ceilings — the most jaw-dropping rooms in Sicily, and pure parents' sacred-geometry bliss. (Shoulders/knees covered.)

    Adults love it · kids amazed · English · federicosecondo.org
  7. 7
    Villa Romana del Casale mosaics
    Piazza Armerina (car only)

    UNESCO Roman floor mosaics — the "bikini girls," a giant animal-hunt scene — on raised, mostly-roofed walkways. Great spot-the-detail for kids.

    Under-18 free · English · on-site
  8. 8
    Cannoli + cooking class
    Taormina / Catania

    Make cannoli (kids 3–12 rate in Taormina) or a full arancini-pasta-cannoli family class with a "minichef" program. Hands-on and delicious.

    Kid rates · English · cookingclasstaormina.com
  9. 9
    Cefalù — beach + Rocca + Norman Duomo
    east of Palermo

    A sandy swimmable beach, a clifftop fortress hike to burn teen energy, and a great cathedral. The perfect easy day near Palermo.

    All ages · beach free
  10. 10
    Capuchin Catacombs
    Palermo

    ~1,250 preserved, dressed mummies — uncanny and unforgettable; squarely the parents' aesthetic and a hit with older boys. Quiet and reverent rather than scary, but the infants' room is intense — brief a sensitive 9-year-old first.

    Adults + older kids · walk-up cash · palermocatacombs.com
— More to do

Deeper cuts

Outdoor & islands

Aeolian Islands day trip

Hydrofoil from Milazzo to Lipari + Vulcano — volcanic islands, swimming, a sulfur crater you can smell. (Lipari+Vulcano by day for the 9-year-old; Stromboli's night lava show is an overnight.)

libertylines.it

Best beaches

San Vito Lo Capo (white sand, shallow — best for kids), Mondello near Palermo, Isola Bella at Taormina (pebbles — bring water shoes).

visitsicily.info

Etna lava-tube cave trek

Helmet-and-headlamp walk into a lava tube with a licensed guide — geology you crawl through. Some routes from age 7.

guidetna.it

Castles, swords & rocks (the boys)

Castello Ursino, Catania

A Swabian fortress (lava reached its walls in 1669) with a medieval-weaponry collection — the closest thing to an armory on the island.

comune.catania.it

Museo dell'Etna, Viagrande

A modern volcanology center with an earthquake simulator and 3D film — hooks both the 9-year-old and the rock-loving teen.

museodelletna.it

Erice + cable car

A medieval mountaintop town reached by cable car, with a Norman cliff castle over an ancient temple of Venus. Far west — pair with the Marsala salt pans.

funiviaerice.it
Malta option A 2-night side trip beats a day trip — and fly, don't ferry: Catania→Malta is ~45 min on Ryanair / KM Malta. With kids: Valletta's Palace Armoury (knights' armor), walled Mdina, and the Neolithic Hypogeum (age 6+, books ~3 months ahead).
Getting in & out In: overnight ferry Naples→Palermo (GNV / Grimaldi, ~10–12 hrs, drive your van on). Out toward the north: the 20-minute Messina→Villa San Giovanni car ferry (no booking) onto the mainland autostrada, or fly from Catania.

Plan it

Book the Naples→Palermo ferry (two cabins for six), Etna (cable car + any guided summit), Streaty Palermo, and any Aeolian boat ahead. Drive — the interior (Villa del Casale) is car-only; park outside town ZTLs.
