— Practical

Law, logistics & the eldest

Italian homeschool law, in brief

Home education ("istruzione parentale") is legal in Italy and growing (15,000+ kids in 2024). For residents it requires an annual notification, a declaration of capacity, and a yearly progress exam. But it's keyed to residency — for a 3-month, multi-base, non-resident stay, families almost always just continue their home-country / online curriculum rather than registering in the Italian system. Confirm your specific case via Controscuola.

Bottom line You'll worldschool under your US framework, not formal Italian istruzione parentale — no Italian registration needed for a non-resident stay.
