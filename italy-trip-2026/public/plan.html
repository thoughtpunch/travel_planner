— How we move

Trains + two ferries

The whole loop is rail except two sea crossings — no mid-trip flights until the very end. Start and end in Rome for the round-trip into FCO.

Sea leg 1

Naples → Palermo

Overnight car/passenger ferry (GNV / Grimaldi, ~10–12 hrs). Drive a rental van on board; two cabins for six. Saves a flight and a hotel night.

Back to the mainland

Sicily → Bologna

The 20-minute Messina→Villa San Giovanni car ferry (no booking) onto the autostrada/rail north — one long transit day that passes Rome.

Sea leg 2 · the fix

Venice → Croatia

No Venice→Croatia ferry runs in November. Real route: train Venice→Ancona, then the year-round overnight Jadrolinija ferry Ancona→Split (Tue/Fri).

The one flight

Croatia → Rome

Fly Dubrovnik→Rome to close the loop (cleanest in November), or ferry Split→Ancona + train. Then the FCO round-trip home on Nov 20.

Why this shape Sicily is the deep south and Croatia the northeast, with Rome in the middle — so a single counter-clockwise loop with the bookend-Rome flights is the only no-backtrack way to fit both. Bologna stays the cheap rail hub (Florence, Venice, Modena, Cremona all reachable).
