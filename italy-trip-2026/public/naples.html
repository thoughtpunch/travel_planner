— The shortlist

Naples Top 10

Family-first, with the grown-up and weird picks marked. EU under-18s are free at Pompeii, Herculaneum, the archaeological museum and Paestum.

  1. 1
    Make real Neapolitan pizza — AVPN
    the official Verace Pizza Napoletana school

    The "pizzaiolo for a day" family course — dough, the wood oven, the 90-second bake, at the source. The summit of the whole trip for a pizza-loving kid.

    Age 5+ · English · book ahead · pizzanapoletana.org
  2. 2
    Pompeii with an archaeologist
    kid-paced family tour

    The buried Roman city, made exciting with clue booklets, the gladiator barracks and baths. Half a day; go early.

    Age 6+ · English · livtours.com
  3. 3
    Mt Vesuvius crater hike
    Gran Cono, Trail 5

    Walk to the rim of the volcano that buried Pompeii — black lava and sulphur vents underfoot. Closed-toe shoes; buy timed tickets only from the official site.

    No min age · vesuviopark.vivaticket.it
  4. 4
    Napoli Sotterranea (underground)
    Centro Storico

    Greek-Roman aqueducts and WWII shelters 40m under the city, with narrow candle-lit passages. Adventurous and a guaranteed kid win.

    All ages · English tours · napolisotterranea.org
  5. 5
    Cappella Sansevero — the Veiled Christ
    Centro Storico · book ahead

    A marble Christ under an impossibly carved "veil," plus an alchemist prince's anatomical machines. Pure Voynich/Escher territory — the parents' must-see.

    Adults + teens · English · museosansevero.it
  6. 6
    Naples Archaeological Museum (MANN)
    the Pompeii treasures

    Where Pompeii's mosaics, frescoes and the giant Farnese marbles actually live (visit before/after the ruins). The "Secret Cabinet" is adults-only Roman erotica — your call.

    Adults + kids · English · mann-napoli.it
  7. 7
    Herculaneum
    Ercolano

    Smaller and better-preserved than Pompeii — wooden beams, upper floors, even carbonized food. Often the kids' favorite of the two.

    All ages · English · ercolano.beniculturali.it
  8. 8
    Spaccanapoli + San Gregorio Armeno
    the old town's straight spine

    The dead-straight street that splits old Naples, the year-round Christmas-figurine alley, street pizza and sfogliatella. Free, vivid, all ages.

    All ages · free wander
  9. 9
    Paestum temples + buffalo mozzarella
    south, toward Salerno

    Three Greek temples better-preserved than anything in Greece, paired with a mozzarella farm (Tenuta Vannulo — watch it pulled by hand). Salerno's beaches are right here.

    All ages · temples English; Vannulo phone-only · tenutavannulo.com
  10. 10
    Amalfi Coast / Capri / Procida day trip
    by ferry or coast road

    Pick one: the Amalfi drive, glamorous Capri, or quieter, colorful Procida. Rhys (18) can do the Circumvesuviana solo.

    All ages · ferries/buses

Deeper cuts

For the rock-loving teen (Jude)

Real Museo Mineralogico

45,000+ minerals and the richest Vesuvius collection anywhere — the perfect primer before the crater hike. €2.50, weekdays.

Real Museo Mineralogico

Carve a cameo — Torre del Greco

Centuries of coral and shell cameo carving; make your own at a historic factory near the volcano.

aucella.com

Solfatara / Campi Flegrei

A whole field of bubbling mud and fumaroles west of the city — active volcanism you can stand in. Check current access (closures happen).

getyourguide.com

Grown-ups love

Pizza crawl — the famous pizzerie

Da Michele, Sorbillo, Di Matteo, 50 Kalò. Eat your way through the contenders; it's cheap and joyful.

cesarine.com

Certosa di San Martino

A Carthusian monastery high over the bay — baroque excess, cloisters, and the best view in Naples. Quiet and adult.

getyourguide.com

Catacombs of San Gennaro

Vast paleo-Christian catacombs run by a local youth co-op — more atmospheric and less crowded than Rome's.

catacombedinapoli.it

Plan it

Book the AVPN course and Cappella Sansevero early. Reserve Vesuvius timed tickets only at vivaticket. Vannulo is phone/email and mornings only.
