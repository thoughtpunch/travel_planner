Croatia — walls & waterfalls

Nov 5–12 · 7 nightsSplit · Dubrovnik · KotorVery English-friendly

Roman palaces, clifftop fortresses, Game-of-Thrones King's Landing, and turquoise waterfalls — ~40% cheaper than Italy. November is deep low season: the mainland (Split, Dubrovnik, Kotor) is fully alive; the islands are not. Read the ferry note first — it changes how you get in.

Getting in — the Venice ferry doesn't run in November The Venice→Croatia catamarans are seasonal (last 2026 sailing ~Oct 13). The real route: train Venice→Ancona, then the year-round overnight Jadrolinija ferry Ancona→Split (Tue/Fri, ~9–11 hrs, book a cabin). To return toward Rome: fly Dubrovnik→Rome (cleanest), or ferry Split→Ancona + train. Confirm exact Nov dates with Jadrolinija.
— The shortlist

Croatia Top 10

Ranked for an early-November week on the mainland. Most sites close by 15:00–17:00 in winter — plan mornings. English is spoken everywhere.

  1. 1
    Klis Fortress
    15 min above Split

    A real medieval clifftop fortress — Game of Thrones' Meereen — with a small armory/weapons exhibit and huge views. The boys' castle jackpot.

    All ages · English · tvrdjavaklis.com
  2. 2
    Diocletian's Palace + cellars
    Split old town

    A living 1,700-year-old Roman palace that is the old town; the cellars were Daenerys's dragon den. Streets are free; the cathedral/bell-tower and cellars are cheap.

    All ages · English · visitsplit.com
  3. 3
    Dubrovnik City Walls
    Dubrovnik old town

    Walk 2 km of sea ramparts around King's Landing. Open year-round (winter ~09:00–15:00); under-18 cheap. ~1,000 steps and some heights — supervise the 9-year-old.

    All ages · English · citywallsdubrovnik.hr
  4. 4
    Kotor + Bay of Kotor day trip (Montenegro)
    ~2 hrs from Dubrovnik

    A fjord-like bay, a walled town, and a 1,350-step fortress climb — plus a new country. The one "island/escape" that actually works in November; off-season border waits are short. Bring passports.

    All ages · English · private driver best
  5. 5
    Game of Thrones walking tour
    Dubrovnik

    15+ King's Landing filming spots; some operators hand out replica swords. Runs year-round. (The Lokrum island add-on is closed in November.)

    Teens + adults · English · gameofthronesdubrovnik.com
  6. 6
    Plitvice Lakes National Park
    inland · day trip

    Turquoise lakes and waterfalls on boardwalks, with autumn foliage and almost no crowds. Winter caveat: boats and shuttle trains don't run and the Upper Lakes are usually closed — it's a Lower-Lakes-on-foot day.

    9-yo OK (lower loop) · English · np-plitvicka-jezera.hr
  7. 7
    Krka National Park (Skradinski buk)
    near Šibenik

    A massive, easy-to-reach waterfall — flatter and shorter than Plitvice, good for the 9-year-old. Enter at Lozovac in winter (transfer boats stop); no swimming.

    All ages · English · np-krka.hr
  8. 8
    Game of Thrones Museum
    Split · indoor

    Real props, costumes and weapons plus an Iron Throne photo — the rainy-day-proof GoT fix for the whole family.

    All ages · English · gameofthronesmuseum.com
  9. 9
    Trogir + Kamerlengo Fortress
    30 min west of Split

    A walled UNESCO island-town with a Venetian fortress to clamber. Town is free to wander; the fortress is cheap (confirm winter hours locally).

    All ages · English · visittrogir.hr
  10. 10
    Pula Roman Arena
    Istria (far north)

    One of the best-preserved Roman amphitheatres, with gladiator underground — open all winter. Only worth it if you route through the north instead of the Split/Dubrovnik core.

    All ages · English · arenapula.hr
— Good to know

November reality + route

What's great vs. closed in early November Great: near-empty A-list sites, deep low-season prices, easy borders, and a fully-alive mainland (Split, Dubrovnik, Kotor). Diminished/closed: the islands largely shut (skip Hvar/Korčula), the Blue Cave on Vis closes Nov 1, and Plitvice/Krka lose their boats and shuttles. Rent a car — off-season bus/ferry logistics are thin.

Suggested 7-day route

Arrive Split by overnight ferry → Diocletian's Palace → Klis + Trogir day → Krka (or Plitvice) → drive to Dubrovnik → Walls + GoT tour → Kotor/Montenegro day → fly Dubrovnik→Rome.

For the boys — caves & rocks

The Blue Cave is closed in Nov; the geology fix is the travertine waterfall systems at Plitvice/Krka and the fortress climbs. Klis + the Split GoT museum cover swords.

np-krka.hr

Montenegro logistics

Dubrovnik→Kotor ~2 hrs each way via Perast; both countries non-Schengen, short off-season border waits. A private driver for six handles the border and is the flexible sweet spot. Euros in Montenegro.

tnp.me

Plan it

Confirm the Nov 2026 Ancona→Split ferry dates + a family cabin with Jadrolinija. Check winter daily hours (most sites close 15:00–17:00). Book a Dubrovnik→Rome flight for the exit. Croatia is very English-friendly throughout.
