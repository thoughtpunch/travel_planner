Daily Rhythm — How a Typical Workday Shapes Up

Dan works 4–11pm Italian local time, M–F. Mornings and early afternoons are family time. Italian dinner culture (8–10pm) means we either eat early as a family before Dan's shift, or Dan steps out for dinner mid-shift since most of his work is calls.

16:00 – 23:00Dan working from the apartment (mostly calls). Family quieter at home or out without him.

Vacation weeks Dan is off work Sept 6 – Sept 20 (the first 2 weeks in Rome). Full presence, no work blocks. One more vacation week TBD — likely the Naples week or final Rome stretch.
Day trip planning note Day trips need to fit in the morning-to-mid-afternoon window (back by 4pm) on weekdays. Weekends are wide open. This is why Bologna is the best base for day trips — most regional destinations are <1h each way, leaving real time on the ground before Dan's shift.

Default: We Do Not Rent a Car

Italian trains are excellent, parking in old cities is brutal, ZTLs (limited-traffic zones) ticket tourists relentlessly, and tolls add up. We rent a car only for:

  • Bomarzo + Civita day trip from Rome (~$60, 1 day)
  • Dolomites weekend from Lido (~$200, 3 days) — Chase Sapphire Preferred covers CDW
  • Maybe Damanhur weekend from Bologna (~$250) — only if combining stops

Grandma's Routing

BUF or ROC → JFK → FCO direct → Frecciarossa direct from FCO Aeroporto station to Bologna Centrale (3h 30m, no transfers). One transatlantic connection. The Frecciarossa is the gentlest part of her trip — she sits the whole way.

Why not fly her directly to Bologna? Connecting through Frankfurt/Munich/Amsterdam adds 2–3 more transfers. The single Frecciarossa from FCO is easier on her body even though it's longer in raw time.

Rome — La Città Eterna

18
Nights
9/6 – 9/24
Ancient Rome Arrival + decompression Pizza al taglio + gelato Weird Lazio day trips

Dan's first vacation block (Sept 6–20). Rhys turns 18 mid-stay. We don't live in tourist Rome — we live in Garbatella, a 1920s garden-city working-class neighborhood with Metro B straight to the Colosseum (10 min). Curving Art Nouveau staircases, internal courtyards, generational Roman families, no tourists, 30–50% cheaper. Filmed in La Grande Bellezza.

Garbatella, Rome · Metro B → Colosseum 10 min

Neighborhood Options

Garbatella — recommended
Metro B → Colosseum 10 min. Working-class, family-friendly. Cesare al Casaletto, Trattoria Augustarello. $3,000–4,500 for 18 nights.
Pigneto
Hip working-class, music + nightlife. Pasolini's old stomping ground. Metro C / Tram. More for parents than kids. Slightly cheaper.
Testaccio
THE Roman food neighborhood (Da Felice, Flavio al Velavevodetto). Walking to Trastevere. More gentrified, prices closer to center.
Bracciano — medieval castle alternative
Medieval lake town with a 15th-c. castle, 1h train. Very cheap. Every Rome trip becomes a 1h+ commute.

The Must-Do List in Rome

Big sites
  • Colosseum + Forum + Palatine — combo ticket, 8:30am entry
  • Vatican Museums + Sistine + St Peter's — 8am early entry, no Wednesdays

  • Catacombs of San Callisto or Domitilla

Voynich / Escher / weird
  • Sala delle Carte Geografiche (Vatican Maps) — 1580 frescoed maps
  • Calcata — medieval hippie village 1h north
  • Bomarzo / Sacro Bosco — Renaissance monster park (day trip)
  • Civita di Bagnoregio — "dying city" on a crumbling pinnacle
  • Tarot Garden (Capalbio) — 22 mosaic Major Arcana sculptures

Home Base Four · Campania

Naples — The Pizza Pilgrimage

14
Nights
11/5 – 11/19
Real Neapolitan pizza Pompeii + Herculaneum Death cult Naples + Escher Amalfi + Capri access

We end the trip in pizza heaven. Naples invented Neapolitan pizza in 1889 (Pizzeria Brandi). The boys eat their way through different historic pizzerias every other night for 14 days. Cheapest of the four bases (30–40% below Rome). Centro Storico is gritty, working-class, medieval — exactly the brief. November weather is mild (mid-50s to mid-60s F).

Spaccanapoli, Naples · the straight road that splits the Centro Storico

Neighborhood Options

Centro Storico (Spaccanapoli) — recommended
Medieval grid, narrow alleys, scooters, pizza joints, churches, shrines. This is the Naples experience. Loud at night — pick high floor.
Quartieri Spagnoli
Formerly rough, now hip. Steep streets, Maradona mural, working-class with hipster gentrification. Loud + cheap.
Vomero
Hilltop residential, calm middle-class families. Funicolare to centro 10 min. Less "Naples experience."
Chiaia
Upscale + elegant near the waterfront. Nicest dining, prettiest walks, safest-feeling. More expensive.

What's in Naples Itself

Big sights
  • Cappella Sansevero — Veiled Christ + Anatomical Machines (real preserved bodies)
  • Napoli Sotterranea — candle-lit Roman aqueducts + WWII bunkers
  • MANN — world's best Greco-Roman collection
  • Three castles (dell'Ovo, Nuovo, Sant'Elmo)
Weird-medieval Naples
  • Fontanelle Cemetery — caves of adopted skulls (still active devotion)
  • Catacombs of San Gennaro
  • Crypts of Santa Maria della Sanità
  • Catacombs of San Gaudioso (frescoed skull niches)
For Kei + Escher
  • Atrani on Amalfi coast — Escher's actual village. He drew the staircases that inspired Relativity from here.
