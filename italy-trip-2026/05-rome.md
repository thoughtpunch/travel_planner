## Neighborhood options

Dan's preference: **NOT touristy central Rome**. Working-class, authentic, walkable, transit-connected. Below ranked by family fit.

### 1. Garbatella — recommended

| | |
|---|---|
| **Vibe** | 1920s garden-city working-class neighborhood, generational Roman families, no tourists |
| **Architecture** | Curving Art Nouveau staircases, 62 "lotti" with internal courtyards, very photogenic |
| **Transit** | Metro B "Garbatella" → Colosseum 10 min, Termini 15 min |
| **Walkability** | Flat, safe, kids in piazze (Piazza Damiano Sauli) |
| **Food** | Cesare al Casaletto, Trattoria Augustarello, Doppiozeroo — all local, no tourist menus |
| **Cost vs center** | 30–50% cheaper |
| **Cultural cred** | Filmed in *La Grande Bellezza*, lots of Pasolini |
| **Downside** | Less spontaneous "step outside into history" feel |

### 2. Pigneto — alternative for hip/edgy

| | |
|---|---|
| **Vibe** | Hip working-class, Pasolini's old neighborhood, music + nightlife |
| **Transit** | Tram 5/14 + Metro C, 15–20 min to center |
| **Walkability** | Flat, very walkable |
| **Food** | Necci dal 1924, Co.So., Caffè Pigneto — hip + cheap |
| **Cost** | Slightly cheaper than Garbatella |
| **Downside** | Some streets edgier at night; more for parents than kids |

### 3. Testaccio — alternative for food

| | |
|---|---|
| **Vibe** | THE Roman food neighborhood, ex-slaughterhouse area, origin of quinto quarto cuisine |
| **Transit** | Metro B "Piramide", walk to Trastevere/Aventine |
| **Food** | Flavio al Velavevodetto, Da Felice, Checchino dal 1887 |
| **Cost** | More gentrified now, prices closer to center |
| **Downside** | More expensive than Garbatella, mercato gets early-tourist now |

### 4. Bracciano — alternative if we want a medieval town outside Rome

| | |
|---|---|
| **Vibe** | Medieval castle town on volcanic crater lake, fairytale-tier |
| **Anchor** | Castello Orsini-Odescalchi (15th c., one of the best-preserved in Italy) |
| **Transit** | 1h 5min train to Rome Ostiense, hourly |
| **Cost** | Very cheap ($1,500–2,500 for 18 nights) |
| **Downside** | Every Rome trip is a 1hr+ commute. Romantic on paper. |

### 5. Orvieto — alternative cliff-city

| | |
|---|---|
| **Vibe** | Medieval cliff city on a tuff plateau, fairytale-tier |
| **Anchor** | 14th-c Duomo with Signorelli's Last Judgment, Underground tunnels |
| **Transit** | Frecciarossa 1h 15m to Rome (expensive); regional 2h |
| **Cost** | Very cheap, all you can want |
| **Downside** | Same as Bracciano — Rome is real planning, not spontaneous |

**Recommendation**: Live in Garbatella as the base, optionally side-trip to Orvieto for 3 nights mid-stay.

## What's in Rome itself

### Big ancient sites
- **Colosseum + Roman Forum + Palatine** combo (book skip-the-line, 8:30am)
- **Castel Sant'Angelo** — papal fortress, dungeon ramps, ramparts — boys' favorite
- **Vatican Museums + Sistine + St Peter's** — book 8am early entry, avoid Wednesdays
- **Catacombs** of San Callisto or Domitilla (book ahead)
- **Capuchin Crypt** — 5 chapels of friars' bones, 30 min visit

### Voynich / Escher / weird-medieval for Kei
- **Sala delle Carte Geografiche** at the Vatican — 40 huge frescoed maps (already on the Vatican route)
- **Calcata** — medieval hippie village 1hr north (intentional community + cliff town)
- **Capuchin Crypt** (also above)
- **Borghese Gardens** — easy park afternoons
- (Side trip to Orvieto for the cliff city + underground tunnels)

### For the boys
- Colosseum (gladiators)
- Castel Sant'Angelo (castle + dungeon)
- Catacombs (skeletons)
- Capuchin Crypt (bones)
- Gelato crawl (Fatamorgana, Giolitti, Otaleg, La Romana)

## What's hard about Rome

- **Cobblestones** are real on bad shoes; bring good walking footwear
- **September is still hot** (75–85°F daytime) — apartment AC matters
- **Mosquitos** in early September, especially near parks
- **Crowds** at major sights — early entry tickets are not optional, they're survival
- **Distances** between sights are bigger than they look on a map — plan transit
